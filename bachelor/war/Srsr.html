QueryBuilder

The first section shows which years of composing were most successful. The success of a year is meassured by the sum of downloaded works. Here either the absolute number of downloads can be considered or the number of downloaded works normalized by how many works had been published in that year. Both versions are shown here. The first image shows the absolute counts, the second image shows the normalized version.
Ages of composers through the times: The nearer to the red end of the color scale the color of a spot is, the more composers had that specific age in a specific year. If you select a genre, a key or a ngram, the results are filtered considering not only the point of time and the age of the composer, but limit them to be able to explorate the ages of composers with regards to genres, keys or melodies. If you want to find out if a melody occurred more often at a certain age of composers and if that changed over time, you can select an ngram and run the query with this limitation. If you want to do even more advanced research, you can combine limitations, for example to see how the results of a limitation to a certain genre change if you additionally specify a certain key.
Countries: How was the frequency of composing in countries over time? The nearer to the red end of the color scale the color of a spot is, the more composing activity was at that point of time in that country. Again you can limit the results if you want to investigate how the composing activity was in different countries if you regard only one genre or key or ngram. If you for example specify a melody you can see when and where it appeared most frequently in composing history.
This section shows the composing activity over the times meassured by absolute numbers of publications.
This section shows the percentage use of keys. You may specify a genre to see in which keys works of that genre are mostly written. You may also specify a melody to see if this melody might appear in works written in a certain key much more than in works written in other keys meaning there is a key that might be specific for the use of a certain melody, although the first intuition is that there is no connection. More easy is the question how the use of keys is in a certain country is.
This section shows the use of keys in different countries. The results are normalized by the sum of published works in a country, so that the use of a key in different countries can be compared and is not influenced by a country that has more published works than another country. The results are the percentages a key is used in a certain country.
This section shows the use of keys over time. You can see for example when the key C was used most frequently. To make sure the comparisons aren`t influenced by the fact that a year had more publications than another, results are normalized by the sum of publications of each year.
This section shows the use of keys among ages of composers. You can see for example at which age of a composer the key C was used most frequently. To make sure the comparisons aren`t influenced by the fact that a composers may produce more works at a certain age than they may at another age, results are normalized by the sum of publications of each age.

This section shows the composing activity of a certain composer over his working period. You can also test at which age the composer composed works of a certain genre or at which age he used a certain key or a melody most frequently.
